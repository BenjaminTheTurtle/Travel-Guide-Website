Welcome to Kalaw!

Kalaw is a charming hill station in northern Shan State, known for its cool climate, scenic landscapes, and ethnic diversity.

Sitting an elevation of around 1,300 meters (4,265 feet), Kalaw offers a refreshing escape from the heat of lower regions and is a popular base for trekking adventures to nearby villages and Inle Lake.

Kalaw Train Station

Kalaw Train Station is the historic railway station serving the hill town of Kalaw. It sits on the scenic line that connects Yangon to Pyin Oo Lwin and further to northern Shan State, including the famous stretch toward Nawnghkio and Aungban. The station retains much of its colonial-era charm, reflecting Kalaw’s history as a British hill station.

Activities to Do

Go Trekking

Kalaw is a trekker’s paradise, with trails leading through forests, hills, and ethnic villages. Popular trekking routes include the Kalaw to Inle Trek and Short day Hikes.

Going for a photoshoot

Kalaw is surrounded by cool pine forests, perfect for walking or photography.

Visit Local Villages

Nearby villages allow visitors to see traditional Shan, Pa-O, and Danu lifestyles while learning about farming, weaving, and local crafts and enjoying cultural interactions and local cuisine.

Travel Advice

Cultural Experiences

Kalaw is home to Shan, Danu, and Pa-O communities, each with unique customs and crafts. Travelers can visit workshops to see weaving, wood carving, and silverwork, or stay in local homestays for an authentic cultural experience. Always ask permission before photographing people, and be respectful of local traditions, especially in villages.

Getting around Kalaw

Kalaw is a small hill town, so most attractions are within walking distance, but you can also rent a bicycle or motorbike to explore nearby villages and hills. Roads are generally paved in town but can get muddy on trails, so wear appropriate footwear. For longer trips, like treks to Inle Lake, consider hiring a local guide or joining a trekking group.
