Welcome to Pindaya!

Pindaya is a serene town nestled in the hills of Shan State, famous for its caves, traditional crafts, and scenic countryside.

Located about 90 km from Taunggyi, Pindaya is a cultural and spiritual hub, attracting visitors with its historic Pindaya Caves, tea plantations, and handicraft villages.

Pindaya Caves

A network of limestone caves filled with over 8,000 Buddha statues in various styles, sizes, and materials. The caves are a major pilgrimage site and a must-see attraction.

Travel Advice

Getting around Pindaya

Pindaya is best explored on foot, bicycle, or motorbike. The caves and lake are within easy reach of the town, but hiring a local guide is helpful for visiting surrounding villages.

Photography & Scenic Tips

Sunrise and late afternoon offer the best light for Pindaya Lake and surrounding hills. Inside the caves, watch for lighting and shadows; bring a camera that performs well in low-light conditions.
