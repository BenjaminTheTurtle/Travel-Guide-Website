Welcome to Taunggyi!

The scenic capital of Shan State, known for its cool mountain climate, cultural diversity, and breathtaking highland views.

Taunggyi sits high above sea level, offering a peaceful escape from the heat. It is most famous for the vibrant Taunggyi Balloon Festival, where colorful hot air balloons light up the sky.

Shwe Bone Pwint Pagoda

One of the most popular places in the city is Shwe Bone Pwint Pagoda, which sits on an elevated area and offers breathtaking panoramic views of the entire city. It is especially beautiful during sunrise and sunset, attracting both locals and visitors seeking a peaceful atmosphere.

Activities to Do

Experience the Taunggyi Balloon Festival

If you’re visiting in November, the Taunggyi Balloon Festival is a must-see. Colorful, massive hot-air balloons are launched into the sky, often decorated with intricate designs. At night, lanterns and fireworks light up the city. Even if you miss the main festival dates, you can often see smaller celebrations and get a sense of the festive spirit.

Shop at Local Markets

Taunggyi has bustling markets that include

  • Aung Myay Thar Zan Market – large local market with fresh produce, textiles, and handicrafts.
  • Street stalls selling Shan snacks and souvenirs – perfect for trying local flavors like tea leaf salad or Shan noodles.

Visit the Taunggyi Lake & Surrounding Hills

The nearby hills and lakes offer scenic escapes from the city. You can-

  • Rent a bicycle or motorbike for a countryside ride.
  • Explore small villages along the way.
  • Enjoy quiet spots for photography or picnics.

Cultural Performances

You can visit small workshops for weaving, silverwork, and pottery while attending cultural shows or local performances if available during festivals.

Travel Advice

Getting Around Taunggyi

Getting around Taunggyi is relatively easy thanks to several transportation options suited for different needs and budgets. The most common way to navigate the city is by using motorbike taxis and cycle rickshaws, which are convenient for short distances and allow you to experience local life up close. For longer trips or travel to nearby towns, shared trucks and minibusses are popular among both locals and tourists. Additionally, car rentals and private taxis are available for those seeking more comfort and flexibility, especially if planning day trips to attractions like Inle Lake or Kakku Pagodas. Walking is also pleasant in the city center, where many sites and markets are close together, making Taunggyi a traveler-friendly destination.

Best time to Visit Taunggyi

The best time to visit Taunggyi is during the cool, dry season, which typically runs from November to February. During these months, the weather is pleasant with mild temperatures and lower humidity, making it ideal for exploring the city and nearby attractions comfortably. This period also coincides with some of Taunggyi’s most vibrant festivals, including the famous Taunggyi Hot Air Balloon Festival held in November, offering visitors a unique cultural experience. Conversely, the rainy season from June to September brings heavy monsoon showers that can make travel more challenging and outdoor activities less enjoyable.
