Visiting Shan State, Myanmar

If you’re looking for a peaceful and beautiful place to explore, Shan State is the perfect choice. Surrounded by mountains and pagodas, it offers a relaxing escape from busy city life. Travelers can visit tourist attractions that range from quiet villages to enormous lakes while enjoying the cold, breezy air of Shan, especially during sunrise and sunset. With its natural beauty, friendly locals, and calm atmosphere, Shan State is a wonderful destination for anyone who wants to slow down and enjoy the journey.

All about Shan State

Surrounded in the eastern highlands of Shan State, this region offers a blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and scenic landscapes. Visitors are drawn to its peculiar hills, traditional villages, and iconic landmarks like the Shwe Bone Pwint Pagoda, where it offers panoramic views of the entire city. Whether you’re exploring bustling local markets or enjoying a quiet sunrise over the mountains, Shan State delivers a peaceful yet enriching travel experience.

Practical Information for Travel

Entry & Safety

Traveling in Shan State requires some awareness of local conditions. While popular destinations such as Inle Lake and Kalaw are generally safe and accessible, certain parts of northern and eastern Shan State may be restricted due to ongoing conflicts. Travelers should check current advisories before visiting remote areas and always carry a passport, as checkpoints are common throughout the region.

Transportation

Getting around Shan State can be an adventure in itself. Most travelers arrive via Heho Airport, which serves as the gateway to Inle Lake. Buses connect major towns and are an affordable option, though journeys can be long and sometimes uncomfortable. Trains offer scenic views of the countryside but are slow, while hiring a private car provides greater flexibility, especially when exploring rural or less accessible areas.

Climate

Shan State enjoys a cooler climate compared to much of Myanmar due to its higher elevation. The best time to visit is during the cool, dry season from November to February, when temperatures are pleasant and skies are clear. The hot season from March to May can be quite warm, while the rainy season from June to October brings lush landscapes but may disrupt travel plans due to poor road conditions.

Money

Cash remains the primary form of payment across Shan State, particularly outside major tourist hubs. ATMs are available in larger towns, but access can be limited in rural areas, so it is advisable to carry sufficient local currency. Mobile coverage is generally available in towns, but internet connectivity may be slow or unreliable, especially in remote regions, so travelers should plan accordingly.

Cultural Respect

Shan State is home to a rich mix of ethnic groups, each with its own traditions and way of life. Visitors can experience vibrant local markets, unique cuisine such as Shan noodles, and traditional village life. Respect for local customs is important such as dressing modestly, removing shoes before entering religious sites, and asking permission before taking photographs. Engaging with local communities respectfully can greatly enrich the travel experience.

Getting to know Shan State

Shan State is the largest state of Myanmar. It covers over 1/4th of Myanmar and is located in the eastern part, bordering neighbouring countries such as China, Laos, and Thailand.

When visiting Shan State, make sure to wear thick clothes that can cover your body as Shan State is usually quite chilly. Additionally, make sure to bring sunscreen and comfortable footwear since Shan is very sunny despite its cold weather.

Some must-see places include the Inle Lake which is known for its panoramic views of the hilly terrain, Shwe Ka Bon Pagoda, and the Pindaya Caves.

There are three major cities in Shan State, Taunggyi, Kengtung, and Lashio. Amongst them, there are also popular travel destinations in the towns of those cities such as Kalaw or Pindaya.

There are some key travel destinations in Shan State such as the Inle Lake, and the Shwe Ka Bon Pagoda as well as festivals such as the hot air balloon Festival. Additionally, ATMs are limited outside of major towns. Therefore, it's best to bring tons of cash with you. Some roads are rough, so expect slow travel.

Must Try Things in Shan State

Shan Noodles

A famous local dish made with rice noodles, usually served with chicken or pork and a light, tasty sauce.

Tofu Nway

A warm and soft tofu dish made from chickpeas. It has a smooth texture and is often eaten with rice noodles or by itself as a comfort food.

Shan Sticky Rice

Rice cooked with turmeric and served with chicken, fish, or pork. It comes with a side of fresh vegetables and a light soup.

Meat Skewers

Meat or vegetables grilled on sticks, often sold in local markets. They are smoky, juicy, and perfect for a quick snack.

Pickled Tea Leaf Salad

A popular dish across Myanmar with unique mix of sour, bitter, and salty flavors. Try the Shan Version too!

Take Panoramic Pictures

Take a snap of yourself with the beauty of Shan's luscious greenery and hills.

Ride the Inle Boat

Riding the Inle Boat to the Pagodas surrounded by water is one of the most relaxing things to do there.

Try the Local Sweet & Sour Strawberries

Shan State produces an abundance of strawberries, they are a must try for their delectable flavour unlike no other.
